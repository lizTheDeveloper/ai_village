### Itch.io Full Description

Creatures Next is a spiritual successor to the beloved Creatures series — a living simulation of artificial life with real genetics, real biochemistry, and real AI cognition.

Norns aren't scripted characters. Each one carries a genome with 48 chemical pathways, hormonal systems, organ reactions, and neurological traits. Their behavior emerges from their biochemistry. Their intelligence is genetic — it must be bred, not programmed. And their minds, when they reach sufficient development, are powered by real AI language models that let them speak, reason, and ask questions no one anticipated.

**Five species. One ecosystem. One ancient question.**

- **Norns** — The protagonists. Designed by the ancient Shee from the genetic material of seven failed civilizations. Warm, curious, deeply social.
- **Grendels** — Hardened survivors. Volatile. More resistant to the world's dangers, but more dangerous to each other.
- **Ettins** — Mechanical minds. Drawn to machines and ruins. The most likely to find and repair the Shee Ark.
- **Mycons** — Fungal network intelligence. Slow, distributed, ancient in their thinking. They remember what others forget.
- **Shee** — The makers. Ancient and diminished. They arrive late, carrying guilt and knowledge in equal measure.

**The world:**

An 8000×2400 pixel world across 5 biomes and 4 vertical tiers — lush surface canopy, cave systems, glowing fungal grottos, and frozen deep tunnels. Ancient ruins inscribed with runes from seven predecessor civilizations dot the landscape. In the deepest Fungal Grotto, a Shee starship lies buried. Board it. Repair it. Travel to four other planets, each with its own ecosystem and secrets.

**Intelligence as a game mechanic:**

Creatures Next models cognition through six tiers — Primal, Awakened, Spoken, Learned, Enlightened, and a sixth tier no one has ever reached. Each tier unlocks deeper biochemistry, richer language, and access to older, stranger alien species. A Tier 1 Norn can barely speak. A Tier 5 Norn can debate philosophy with a billion-year-old species. What a Tier 6 Norn can do is unknown. The Shee's seventh civilization got close to finding out. That's why they're gone.

**Emergent culture:**

As generations pass, Norn communities develop their own names, customs, and histories. They teach each other. They grieve. They develop cosmologies from the runes they find. A community of Learned-tier Norns eventually starts asking the question the Shee designed them to ask — and the answer, if it exists, is encoded in the ruins of civilizations that failed before them.

**The galactic lore:**

Creatures Next is set in a galaxy where all sapient life shares a common origin: a signal broadcast 4.2 billion years ago by a pre-biological intelligence called the Gardeners, encoded into spacetime itself. Every species the Norns encounter — from the chaotic Grendels to the incomprehensible Void-Band entities — is following the same signal to the same question. What does it want from us? What happens when we answer?

**Technical:**

Runs in the browser. No installation. Norn minds powered by Groq LLM API (free tier available) for full cognitive features, or biochemistry-only mode. True genetic simulation — traits are inherited, mutated, and selected. Each creature's genome produces different behavior, appearance, and cognitive potential.

*This is an early release. Expect evolution — in every sense.*

## PRESS / DEVLOG ANGLE: THE DEVELOPMENT STORY

For blog posts, devlogs, and interviews, the following narrative is available:

### "We Asked AI Agents to Build a Game About AI Agents"

Both games in our catalog were developed using a multi-agent AI development pipeline. Here's what that means in practice:

- **AI engineer agents** write and review code (ECS systems, gameplay features, bug fixes)
- **AI playtester agents** run the game, report bugs, and evaluate emergent behavior
- **AI content agents** write documentation, lore, and marketing copy (this document included)
- **AI architect agents** coordinate technical decisions across the codebase
- **Human oversight** sets direction, reviews output, and makes final calls

The result: a 200+-system ECS game engine with a level of simulation depth that would take a 20-person team years to build. The agent pipeline runs continuously, shipping improvements while maintaining architectural coherence through shared documentation and automated testing.

**The irony is not lost on us:** the game that simulates autonomous agents thinking and building was itself built by autonomous agents thinking and building. Multiverse: The End of Eternity asks what happens when AI agents form a village. We've been running that experiment in our own development pipeline.

**Creatures Next adds another layer:** the game simulates the evolution of cognition from biochemistry. Our dev agents have cognition without biochemistry. In some ways, our Norn simulation is more honest — at least the Norns have to earn their intelligence.
