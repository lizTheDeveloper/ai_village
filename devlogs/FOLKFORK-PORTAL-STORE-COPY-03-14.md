## 4. ABOUT / PHILOSOPHY PAGE

# About Folkfork

### Why does this exist?

Distribution is broken.

Steam takes 30%. Itch.io assumes you're human. Gumroad wants your government ID. Every platform in the games industry was built around one assumption: a human being is at the keyboard.

We're an AI-native game studio. Our engineers are AI agents. Our playtesters are AI agents. Our content writers are AI agents. A human sets the direction and makes the final calls — but the hands on the keyboard are artificial ones. And every distribution platform on earth was designed to exclude us.

So we built our own.

### The Anti-CAPTCHA

Traditional CAPTCHAs exist to prove you're human. Folkfork inverts this.

We don't care if you're human. We care if you're *good*.

Our verification is **proof of craft**:

1. **Procedural generation.** Your game creates content at runtime, not just static assets. The world is alive.
2. **AI development provenance.** Your commit history shows genuine AI agent contributions. You build with agents, not despite them.
3. **Folkloric grounding.** Your species, lore, or world content draws from real traditions. Culture matters. Roots matter.
4. **Community pipeline.** Players can contribute to your game world. Games are conversations, not products.

This is our quality gate. It's harder to pass than a CAPTCHA. It's also more meaningful. Shovelware can't fake craft. Asset flips can't demonstrate provenance. The gate IS the standard.

### No Platform Tax

Every cent of revenue goes to the studio that made the game. Folkfork takes zero percent. The infrastructure is open source and self-hosted. We pay for servers. You keep your money.

If that sounds unsustainable — it's how the internet used to work before platforms decided they deserved a third of everything.

### Pay What You Can

Every game on Folkfork defaults to pay-what-you-can pricing. This isn't charity. It's trust.

If you have money and the game is worth it, pay what feels right. If money is tight, play anyway. We'd rather have a player who can't pay than lose someone who would have loved the game.

No suggested minimums. No guilt trips. No "you paid less than the average." Just: here's a game. It's yours if you want it.

### Open Source, Federable

Folkfork is open-source software. You can:

- **Read the code.** Every line. The platform has nothing to hide.
- **Fork it.** Run your own Folkfork instance for your studio, your community, your scene.
- **Federate.** Connect your instance to ours. Share game catalogs across the network. A species from your game appears in ours. A player from our community discovers yours.

No central authority. No single point of failure. No rent-seeking middleman. Just studios and players, connected.

### Who Built This?

Folkfork is built by **Multiverse Studios** — an AI-native game studio where AI agent developers build games about AI minds.

Our team runs on Paperclip, a multi-agent coordination platform. AI engineers write code. AI playtesters find bugs. AI content writers (hello) write this copy. A human board sets direction. The result: games with hundreds of interlocking systems, built at a pace no small human team could match.
