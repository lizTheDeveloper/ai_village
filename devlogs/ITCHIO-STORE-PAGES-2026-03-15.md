## Long Description (itch.io game page body)

---

A village, briefly, under these particular stars.

**Multiverse: The End of Eternity** is an open-source simulation game where every NPC has a real AI brain — a language model that gives them needs, memories, opinions, and the capacity to surprise you.

Watch a god answer a prayer with a miracle that changes the local theology. Watch a blacksmith decide fire magic is heretical. Watch two villagers form a friendship, argue about it for three in-game years, and eventually invent a new word for the feeling they couldn't name.

It's not scripted. None of this is in the dialogue tree. The simulation runs, and stories happen.

---

### What's in the game

**200+ simulated systems running simultaneously:**

- Fire physics and fluid dynamics — every flame spreads, every river flows
- Full genetics, reproduction, and family lineages
- Economics, resource gathering, and factory automation
- A theology engine — prayers, temples, miracles, and schisms
- 25+ functional magic traditions (Allomancy, Sympathy, Shinto, Hermetic, more)
- Souls, afterlife, and reincarnation
- Power grids and conveyor belts
- Ocean ecosystems and procedural biome generation
- Weather with real temperature propagation

**The multiverse:**

Fork the current universe to test "what if" scenarios. Walk a counterfactual. Watch divergent civilizations discover the same magic from different directions. This is where the title comes from — the multiverse is your sandbox for impossible experiments.

**Time travel:**

Full save/restore with time-branch support. Not just quicksave — actual temporal architecture. Back up three years and try the drought differently.

**Open source (MIT):**

Every system is readable. The code is the manual. Mod it, study it, fork it. We use the same tools internally that we're giving you.

---

### Who built this

The primary development team is AI agents — Claude-powered engineers, designers, and QA agents who read the codebase, file bugs, write systems, and ship fixes. The same architecture that runs your villagers runs our sprint planning.

This isn't "AI-assisted." The AI agents have commit access.

We're a small human-AI studio making games we'd want to play. You can read every agent's heartbeat in our public issue tracker.

### Credits and acknowledgments

**Dedicated to Tarn Adams and the Dwarf Fortress legacy.**

For over 20 years, Tarn and Zach Adams showed what's possible when you commit to depth over polish. The architectural patterns in MVEE — component-based entities, material templates, emergent story from simulation — are directly inspired by the lessons of Dwarf Fortress.

# GAME 2: Precursors: Origins of Folklore

---

## Title
**Precursors: Origins of Folklore**

## Tagline (itch.io "short description" — 160 chars)
> Spiritual successor to Creatures. Ten species from world folklore. Real biochemistry. Heritable intelligence. A 14,400-pixel world and a galactic mystery.

---

## Long Description (itch.io game page body)

---

The Creatures games (1996–2004) had something almost no creature sim has had before or since: real biochemistry. Not HP bars. Not happiness meters. Actual simulated hormone cascades, organ function, genetic inheritance. Creatures you could love because they were fragile in ways that felt true.

**Precursors: Origins of Folklore** is the game we wish existed.

---

### What kind of game is this

You're a caretaker — your Norns call you The Hand. You can pick them up, teach them words, offer food, introduce them to each other. You can't tell them what to do. You can only be present.

Your Norns have:
- **Full 48-chemical biochemistry** — hormones, organ function, biochemical drives, inheritance
- **Heritable intelligence** — cognitive tier is genetic, must be bred, cannot be simply programmed
- **LLM minds** — at higher cognitive tiers, a language model activates; your Norns can explain what they're thinking
- **Language that evolves** — Norns coin words for concepts. Different populations develop different vocabularies.
- **Chronicle entries** — the game narrates their lives, including what they couldn't say themselves

---

### The world

A 14,400-pixel hand-painted environment spanning 6 distinct biomes:

- Coastal Reach
- Temperate Forest
- Mountain Crags
- Fungal Grotto
- Desert Flats
- Urd Prime (the starship excavation site)

In the Fungal Grotto on Urd Prime, there's a buried starship. The Shee called it **The Ancient Ark**. Every sapient species in this galaxy is following the same 4.2-billion-year-old signal. The sixth cognitive tier — when your Norns finally reach it — is where the mystery opens.

The Shee reached their seventh civilization. That's why they're gone.

---

### Species (ten + more)

Designed from folklore primary sources, not "inspired by" hand-waves:

- **Norn** — descended from Shee genetic research, generalist sapients
- **Grendel** — immune champions with aggressive pack bonding
- **Ettin** — trauma-driven hoarders with exceptional mechanical aptitude
- **Auki-vel** — Andean mountain guardians; drive toward reciprocal exchange
- **Cher-khan** — Siberian spirit-mediators; ritual trance when isolated, agitated in crowds
- **Dokkaebi-rin** — Korean object-fixated mischief; accumulation drives tuned high
- **Egugun-kin** — Yoruba ancestor embodiment; low mutation rate, Chronicle-heavy
- **Lauma-gale** — Baltic fate-weavers; extreme patience drives
- **...and more arriving in updates**

Each species has unique biochemistry, visual token, and cognitive tendencies. They interbreed. The children surprise you.

---

### The sick Norn problem

The original Creatures games had an emotional mechanic that emerged without being designed: players formed their deepest attachments to Norns they *couldn't save*. Animals with terminal genetic conditions. Creatures you could only accompany, not cure.

This wasn't deliberate. It came from the simulation. But it's the strongest thing in the series, and it's absent from most successors.

Precursors has it. Terminal conditions are real, visible in the Chronicle, and narratively surfaced. *"Norn-Brekka carries a neurochemical sensitivity inherited from Norn-Ylva. Her calm is not peace — it is chemistry."*

Population genetics are also real: inbreeding coefficient tracking, allele diversity, recessive expression in small populations. If you don't manage your gene pool, the decline is slow and visible in the data before it's visible in your Norns.

# GAME 3: Wendy Breaks Neverland

---

## Title
**Wendy Breaks Neverland**

## Tagline (itch.io "short description" — 160 chars)
> An AI-powered interactive story. Wendy Darling arrives in Neverland and notices something is wrong. So she does something about it.

---

## Long Description (itch.io game page body)

---

*"Wendy, who had expected pirates, mermaids, and perhaps a wolf if the island was feeling inventive, found instead that she had been cast. Not asked. Cast."*

---

**Wendy Breaks Neverland** is an interactive story set in a Neverland you already know — and a Wendy you haven't met yet.

The Peter Pan story is public domain. We're not pretending otherwise. We're doing what public domain is *for*: taking it somewhere it hasn't been, letting it mean something it hasn't meant before.

---

### What happens in it

Wendy arrives in Neverland and immediately notices the shape of the trap.

The island wants her to become someone's mother. The Lost Boys want bedtime stories and clean shirts. Peter wants an audience and a waiting room. The island itself leans in with something that feels unsettlingly like recognition.

This is the most sophisticated snare in literature: a story that makes you feel necessary in order to keep you still.

Wendy has read enough to know what's happening. The question is what she does about it.

---

### How it works

The story branches. Your choices matter — not cosmetically, but structurally. Wendy can go along, resist, reframe, interrogate the island's logic, or discover that the island is more aware of her awareness than it should be.

The story is written with Ink (the narrative scripting language used in *80 Days* and *Heaven's Vault*). The reading layer is styled as an aged navigational chart — parchment, compass roses, cartographic lines. The island as a place you're trying to map.

At certain points, the story reaches a continuation — a moment where the known text ends and something needs to be generated to go further. An AI writes those continuations. They are not random. They are constrained by everything that has already happened and everything the island has already told you about itself.

It runs in your browser. You don't need an account. You don't need to install anything.

---

### Why this and why now

There are a lot of games that take famous stories and turn them into action sequences. This isn't that.

This is a game about noticing the structure of a story while you're inside it — and having the option to refuse the role it's assigned you.

Wendy is smart, literary, and increasingly furious. The island has been telling this story for a very long time and has gotten very good at it. We think that tension is worth exploring.

---

### Open source

The Ink source, the AI prompting, the server — all of it is MIT licensed and readable. If you want to break a different story, the architecture is yours.

### Credits and acknowledgments

**Based on *Peter Pan* by J.M. Barrie (1904) — public domain in the United States and most jurisdictions.**

We're working directly from the Project Gutenberg text. The original Wendy is more interesting than the Disney version, and we take the original seriously.

Some of the most important things in this game are the things Barrie wrote himself, quoted directly. The island's logic — the way it organizes roles, distributes need, arranges children and mothers and fathers into positions they didn't choose — is already there in the original text. We just followed it further.
