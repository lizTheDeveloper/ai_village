## The Starting Experience

*Your first game: A deity-forbidden world*

### The Forbidden World

**Opening Scenario:**
You start in a **null-magic world** - a universe where the Supreme Creator has **forbidden all mortal magic**.

**Visual Aesthetic:**
- Looks like Dwarf Fortress in medieval setting
- No visible magical entities at first
- Low-fantasy medieval simulation
- Mundane, grounded, realistic

**The Prohibition:**
- Supreme Creator has **decreed: "Magic is forbidden to mortals"**
- Divine law enforced through surveillance
- Creator rarely manifests but watches from afar
- Other gods may exist but practice **only god magic** (hidden from mortals)
- No human/mortal magic allowed

### The Rare Exceptions

**Magical Items Appear:**
Despite the prohibition, **occasionally magical artifacts manifest**:
- Ancient relics from before the ban
- Divine accidents (gods losing items)
- Forbidden creations smuggled into world
- Anomalies the Creator missed

**Discovery Moment:**
```
Your follower finds a glowing sword in ancient ruins.
They bring it to the temple.
The sword pulses with forbidden power.

What do you tell them to do?
- Hide it (risk detection)
- Destroy it (lose power)
- Use it carefully (tempt fate)
- Return it to ruins (play it safe)
```

### The Catalyst: Creator Intervention

**The Too-Powerful Artifact:**
Eventually, your followers acquire or use an artifact **too powerful to ignore**.

**Creator's Arrival:**
```
The sky tears open.

SUPREME CREATOR manifests.

"Magic is FORBIDDEN in this world.
I will be smiting your follower."

Your most devoted follower screams as divine fire consumes them.
The artifact shatters into dust.

The Creator's gaze turns to YOU.

"You allowed this. You are warned."

The sky seals. The Creator vanishes.
Your follower is dead.
```

### The Choice Moment

**Your First True Decision:**

You are a god. The Creator just:
- Killed your follower
- Destroyed a source of power
- Threatened you
- Enforced tyrannical law on YOUR mortals

**Choose Your Path:**

**1. Bow to the Creator (Loyalist Path)**
- Accept the prohibition
- Enforce the ban yourself
- Become a "lawful" god
- Join Creator's surveillance network
- Safer but constrained

**2. Side with Your Followers (Rebel Path)**
- Reject the prohibition
- Secret magical items allowed
- Begin quiet resistance
- Risk Creator's wrath
- Dangerous but free

**3. Hidden Third Path (Trickster Path)**
- Publicly obey, privately rebel
- Teach followers to hide magic
- Disguise artifacts as mundane
- Play the long game
- Riskiest but most rewarding

### Starting Options

**Option A: Start as God**
- Begin as a nascent deity
- Immediately face the prohibition
- Gather followers in deity-forbidden world
- Experience Creator intervention firsthand
- Must choose stance early

**Option B: Start as Mortal (with Avatar)**
- **Use existing avatar system**
- Play as mortal villager/agent
- Discover magical artifact yourself
- Witness Creator's intervention from mortal perspective
- **Die permanently** (no time travel yet!)
- Upon death OR heroic ascension ‚Üí become god
- Remember mortal experience as god
- Deeper emotional connection to followers

**Hardcore Mode (Mortal Start):**

## Union Magic

*Transformative magic through intimate union - Mystif-inspired*

### Concept
Sexual/romantic union as a source of transformative magical power. Bypasses biological reproduction through pure magical creation.

### Key Features

**Union Spells:**
- `merge_forms` - Temporarily merge two beings into one
- `create_offspring` - Birth magical offspring (not genetic)
- `become_desire` - Mystif-style shapeshifting to partner's ideal
- `swap_traits` - Exchange physical/mental attributes
- `soul_bond` - Permanent connection between beings

**Magic Costs:**
- **Intimacy** (new cost type) - Vulnerability/openness
- **Identity** (new cost type) - Risk of losing self in union

**Mechanics:**
- Power scales with trust and intimacy between participants
- Requires mutual consent (cannot force)
- Can create offspring beyond biological genetics
- Risk: Identity bleed (partners begin thinking as one)

### Integration
- Works with existing MagicSystem
- Hooks into ReproductionSystem for magical offspring
- Creates transformer deities (shapeshifters)

## Cosmic Rebellion

*Divine civil war against a tyrannical Supreme Creator*

### Deity Tier System

```
supreme_creator    ‚Üí The first god (tyrannical)
primordial         ‚Üí Ancient gods
pantheon_god       ‚Üí Standard gods
ascended_mortal    ‚Üí Mortals who achieved divinity
rebel_god          ‚Üí Gods opposing Creator
bound_god          ‚Üí Imprisoned by Creator
transformer        ‚Üí Mystif-style shapeshifters
divine_servant     ‚Üí Angels, messengers
```

### Supreme Creator Component

**Tyranny Profile:**
- `controlLevel` - How tightly controls creation
- `paranoia` - Fear of rebellion
- `wrathfulness` - Punishment severity
- `isolation` - Distance from creation

**Forbidden Knowledge:**
- Secrets the Creator hides from mortals
- Weaknesses (discoverable by rebels)
- True origin story vs propaganda

**Surveillance:**
- Watches all prayers
- Has spy gods reporting
- Awareness level of rebellion

### Rebellion Progression

**Discovery Phase:**
- God witnesses Creator's injustice
- Discovers forbidden knowledge
- Learns Creator's weakness

**Conspiracy Phase:**
- Secret meetings with other gods
- Recruit allies carefully
- Awaken mortals to the truth
- Risk: Creator detection

**Escalation Phase:**
- Open rebellion declared
- Divine civil war
- Realm liberation
- Mortal involvement

**Endgame:**
- Exploit Creator's weakness
- Attempt reconciliation OR overthrow
- Victory conditions OR eternal stalemate

### Detection Mechanics

**Creator Surveillance:**
- Each rebellion activity has detection risk
- Paranoid creators detect more easily
- Spy gods increase risk dramatically
- Public dissent = almost certain detection

**Creator Response Stages:**
1. **Suspicious** - Increase surveillance
2. **Investigating** - Send loyal gods
3. **Cracking Down** - Punish exposed rebels
4. **Purge** - All-out divine war

### Mortal Involvement

**Awakened Mortals:**
- Learn forbidden truths from rebel gods
- Can spread truth, shelter rebels, sabotage
- Risk Creator punishment
- Martyrdom = massive rebellion boost

## Mortal Ascension

*Greek mythology-style paths to divinity*

### Ascension Paths

1. **Heroic Deeds** (Heracles) - 12+ legendary accomplishments
2. **Divine Favor** (Ganymede) - God elevates you
3. **Sacred Union** (Psyche & Eros) - Marriage with deity
4. **Martyrdom** (Saints) - Die for sacred cause
5. **Divine Consumption** (Ambrosia) - Eat divine substances
6. **Theft** (Prometheus) - Steal divinity
7. **Rebellion Reward** - Granted for aiding rebellion
8. **Demigod Ascent** (Dionysus) - Divine parentage manifests
9. **Transcendence** (Buddha) - Perfect enlightenment

### Requirements

Each path has specific requirements:
- Power/stat thresholds
- Heroic deed count
- Divine relationships
- Special items/events
- Often requires death

### The Experience

**Before Unlock:**
- Death is permanent
- No ascension possible
- Stuck as mortal

**Unlocking Ascension:**
1. Meet requirements
2. Attempt ascension (can fail!)
3. If successful: Transform to deity
4. Become `ascended_mortal` tier god

### Ascended God Traits

**What Makes Them Different:**
- Remember mortal life (empathy for mortals)
- Natural rebel sympathizers (remember oppression)
- Weaker than primordial gods initially
- Caught between worlds (gods see as lesser, mortals as other)

**Narrative Hooks:**
- Watch mortal loved ones age and die
- Struggle with immortality burden
- Champion mortal causes
- Often join rebellions

## Research Questions

- **Save System Integration:** How to gracefully integrate time travel with existing save/load without breaking it? Use wrapper or direct modification?
- **Timeline Forking:** How to represent multiple timelines in UI? Tree view, list, or timeline graph?
- **Orchestrator Security:** How to safely allow source code editing without breaking game? Sandboxing or validation?
- **God Chat Room:** Use real multiplayer or simulated AI gods? Performance and complexity tradeoffs?
- **4D/5D Rendering:** How to visualize higher-dimensional spaces in 2D/isometric view? Use projections or abstract representations?
- **Meta-Progression Storage:** Store in separate file or main save? Prevent loss if main save corrupted?
- **Mortal ‚Üí God Transition:** Smooth transition or dramatic cutscene? Balance narrative impact with gameplay flow
- **Creator AI:** How intelligent should supreme creator AI be? Too smart = unbeatable, too dumb = unrealistic
- **Universe Quirks:** How many quirks make universe interesting vs overwhelming? Test 1, 3, 5 quirk configurations
- **Impossibility Spaces:** How to define "impossible" geometry in code? Use special rendering or data structures?

## Performance Considerations

- **Timeline History:** Cap timeline history entries to prevent memory bloat (max 1000 timelines)
- **Universe Count:** Limit active universes (suggest 10 max simultaneously loaded)
- **Meta-Progression:** Store only essential data, compress historical timelines
- **God Chat Room:** Cache messages, paginate history, limit active connections
- **Orchestrator Dashboard:** Lazy-load panels, only render active panel
- **Source Code Access:** Don't load entire codebase, load on-demand by file
- **Spatial Dimensions:** 4D/5D rendering uses simplified projections, not full calculations
